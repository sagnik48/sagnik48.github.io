$\mathrm{GL}_2$-Automorphic forms:

Detailed studies of $\mathrm{GL}_2$-automorphic forms were carried out by Herve Jacquet and Robert Langlands. Here are some of the useful references that I used to learn this:

  • Notes from Ngo's lecture on this topic in 2011 at University of Chicago. This also bridges the classical modular forms with $\mathrm{GL}_2$-Automorphic forms.

  • Roger Godement's notes on Jacquet-Langlands' Theory. His approach is quite classical and explicit.

Representation of $p$-adic Groups:

There are very few (possibly no English article/book) which could cover all the aspects of the Representation theory $p-$adic Groups. However, following articles can serve as quite a good source of "introdution" to this topic.

  • The 1976 Representations of the group $\mathrm{GL}_n(F)$ where $F$ is a Non-archimedean Local Field by J.Bernstein and A.Zelevinsky. Covers most of the important and useful introductory aspects of Representation of $\mathrm{GL}_n(F)$. One of the main component of this article is to geometrize representation of $p$-adic groups and then use it to classify the representations (which is done in two other papers of Bernstein-Zelevinsky)

  • Casselman's Notes . Extremely well-written and detailed. Especially if one wants to learn about the intertwining operators. Also, covers many introductory results.

  • Stephen DeBacker's Notes . Covers all the introductory facts and also has detailed explanation of Bernstein Center, Casselman's Square-Integrability theorem, etc.

  • Yihang Zhu's Notes .

  • Tadic's Classification of unitary representations in irreducible representations of general linear group (non-Archimedean case) .

Using "Derivatives" Bernstein-Zelevinsky proved that every square-integrable representations are induced from supercuspidal representations and every generic representation of $\mathrm{GL}_n(F)$ are induced from essentially-square integrable representations. In particular, to every generic representation one can attach a Bernstein-Zelevinsky datum, based on how the representation is induced. This is precisely what one calls a "Bernstein-Zelevinsky" classification. The only references for this topic are:

  • Bernstein and Zelevinsky's 1977 Induced Representation of reductive $p$-adic Groups. I

  • Zelevinsky's 1980 Induced Representation of reductive $p$-adic Groups. II
